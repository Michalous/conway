=== Interpretation ===

This data represents the number of generations until a cycle is detected in random 30x30 Game of Life grids starting from 50/50 alive/dead probability.

The average cycle detection takes about 209 steps.

The distribution is right-skewed, meaning most simulations find cycles relatively quickly, but some take much longer (outliers).

The Q-Q plot shows deviation from normality, particularly in the tails.



=== Visual Analysis ===

1. Histogram with KDE: Clear right-skew with a long tail of long cycles

2. Box Plot: Confirms the presence of many high-value outliers

3. Q-Q Plot: Significant deviation from normal distribution, especially in tails

4. CDF: Shows that ~75% of simulations complete within 264 steps



=== Key Insights ===

  • Most Game of Life patterns stabilize into cycles relatively quickly (median 175 steps)
  • However, some configurations are remarkably persistent, taking over 1,000 generations
  • The distribution is heavily skewed, suggesting that while typical behaviour is predictable, extreme cases exist
  • This aligns with the complex, chaotic nature of Conway's Game of Life

The data suggests that random initial conditions in Game of Life typically lead to cycle detection with a few hundred steps, but the system can exhibit surprising longevity in certain configurations.
